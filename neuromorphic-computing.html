Project Overview

Our neuromorphic computing research focuses on developing revolutionary brain-inspired computing architectures that leverage the unique properties of photonics to achieve unprecedented energy efficiency and processing speeds.

By mimicking the neural structures and processing mechanisms of the human brain, our photonic neuromorphic systems can perform complex cognitive tasks while consuming orders of magnitude less power than traditional computing architectures.

Ultra-low power consumption: 1000x more efficient than traditional processors

Real-time learning and adaptation capabilities

Massively parallel processing with optical interconnects

Key Technologies

Photonic Synapses

Optical waveguides that mimic biological synapses, enabling weighted connections between photonic neurons with femtojoule energy consumption.

  • • Phase-change materials for synaptic plasticity
  • • Non-volatile weight storage
  • • Wavelength division multiplexing

Spiking Neural Networks

Event-driven processing using optical spikes that closely emulate biological neural communication patterns.

  • • Temporal coding schemes
  • • Asynchronous computation
  • • Spike-timing dependent plasticity

Photonic Reservoirs

Complex optical networks that provide rich dynamics for reservoir computing applications.

  • • Delay-based architectures
  • • Nonlinear optical dynamics
  • • High-dimensional state spaces
