Getting Started with GAIA

Follow these steps to set up GAIA in your environment

1

Install GAIA Controller

Deploy the GAIA controller in your Kubernetes cluster using Helm or kubectl.

helm install gaia gaia/gaia --namespace gaia-system
2

Configure Energy Monitoring

Set up energy monitoring for your nodes and workloads.

kubectl apply -f energy-monitoring.yaml
3

Access Dashboard

Launch the GAIA dashboard to monitor and control your energy optimization.

Launch Dashboard

Prerequisites

Kubernetes cluster (v1.20+)
Helm 3.x installed
kubectl configured
Node energy monitoring enabled
Minimum 2 CPU cores per node
4GB RAM per node

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about GAIA

How much energy can GAIA save?

GAIA typically reduces energy consumption by 15-30% depending on your workload patterns and infrastructure configuration. Most customers see savings of 20-25%.

Does GAIA affect application performance?

No, GAIA is designed to optimize energy consumption without impacting application performance. Our algorithms prioritize maintaining SLA requirements.

What Kubernetes versions are supported?

GAIA supports Kubernetes versions 1.20 and above. We test extensively with the latest stable releases and provide compatibility updates.

Can I use GAIA with cloud providers?

Yes, GAIA works with all major cloud providers including AWS, GCP, Azure, and on-premise Kubernetes distributions.

How does carbon-aware scheduling work?

GAIA integrates with renewable energy APIs to schedule workloads during periods of high renewable energy availability, reducing your carbon footprint.

Is there a free trial available?

Yes, we offer a 14-day free trial for the Professional plan. No credit card required to get started.
