Advanced competitive material design platform leveraging Large Language Models for accelerated materials discovery and device optimization

Platform Overview

Our AI/LLM-Assisted Design platform represents a paradigm shift in materials discovery and device optimization. By combining state-of-the-art language models with domain-specific materials science knowledge, we enable researchers to accelerate the development cycle from years to weeks.

The platform integrates literature mining, knowledge graph construction, predictive modeling, and inverse design capabilities into a unified system that can parse scientific literature, extract synthesis recipes, predict material properties, and generate novel material designs optimized for specific applications.

Key Achievement:

Building on ITMO's breakthrough of 81% accuracy in predicting nanoparticle shapes using GPT-4, our platform extends these capabilities to full materials design workflows.

Data Schemas

material_entity.yaml

material:
  name: "Au nanorod"
  composition: "Au"
  structure:
    type: "nanorod"
    aspect_ratio: 3.6
  ligands: ["CTAB", "Ag+"]
  target_properties:
    - name: "LSPR_longitudinal_nm"
      value: 800
      tol: 20

synthesis_step.yaml

step:
  method: "seed-mediated growth"
  reagents:
    - {name: "HAuCl4", conc_mM: 0.5}
    - {name: "CTAB", conc_mM: 100}
    - {name: "AgNO3", conc_mM: 0.1}
    - {name: "ascorbic acid", conc_mM: 0.64}
  conditions:
    T_C: 28
    pH: 2.5
    time_min: 30
  characterization: ["UV-Vis", "TEM"]
  outcomes:
    morphology: "rod"
    AR: 3.5-3.8
    LSPR_nm: 780-820

Example Workflows

A. Text-Only → Predicted Morphology

User Input:

"Seed-mediated Au in CTAB + Ag+ co-ion; mild AA; 25–30 °C"

System Output:

"Predicted: Rod morphology, AR≈3–4 (81% confidence)
Suggested validation: UV-Vis (LSPR 760–820 nm), TEM"

B. Inverse Design (Plasmonic Target)

Goal:

800 nm LSPR in water with Au nanorods

Planner Output:

  • • Seed recipe with CTAB/AgNO₃/AA parameter ranges
  • • 12-run DOE optimizing AR↔LSPR relationship
  • • Gans-surrogate model + U-Net size/AR validation

Best Result:

T=28°C, [Ag+] 0.08–0.12 mM, AA 0.6–0.7 mM
Uncertainty bands: ±15 nm LSPR, ±0.2 AR

Example Prompts

Literature Scout

You are a nanomaterials librarian. Given a target "Au nanorods with 800nm LSPR", return 8 most relevant recipes with reagents, temperatures, times, pH, and reported morphology or property, normalized units. Output YAML list with fields: source_doi, method, reagents[], conditions{}, outcome{}.

Design-to-Target

You are a synthesis planner. Target: TiO2 nanoparticles, 20nm, anatase phase. Constraints: aqueous synthesis, <100°C, non-toxic precursors. Propose: (1) 6 candidate recipes; (2) a 12-run DOE table; (3) risks & mitigations; (4) measurement plan.

Image-in-the-Loop

Given TEM masks + scale bar, compute size/AR distributions and decide if design met spec: "rods with AR 3.5±0.5, length 40±5nm". Return pass/fail + confidence and what to tweak next.
