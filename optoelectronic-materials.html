Optoelectronic Materials

Advanced III-V semiconductors, GaN, InP, AlN, and quantum dot devices for next-generation photonic and electronic applications

Materials Overview

Our optoelectronic materials research focuses on the development and optimization of advanced compound semiconductors and nanostructures. These materials form the foundation for high-performance devices in communications, sensing, power electronics, and quantum technologies.

By leveraging cutting-edge epitaxial growth techniques and precise material engineering, we create tailored heterostructures with exceptional optical and electronic properties for specific applications.

Core Materials

Gallium Nitride (GaN)

  • • Wide bandgap (3.4 eV) for high-power applications
  • • High electron mobility and saturation velocity
  • • Excellent thermal stability up to 1000°C
  • • Blue/UV LEDs and laser diodes
  • • RF power amplifiers for 5G/6G
  • • High-voltage power switching devices

Key Applications: Power electronics, RF devices, LED lighting, UV detectors

Indium Phosphide (InP)

  • • Direct bandgap (1.34 eV) ideal for telecom wavelengths
  • • Superior electron velocity and mobility
  • • Low noise characteristics
  • • Photonic integrated circuits (PICs)
  • • High-speed photodetectors and modulators
  • • Quantum cascade lasers

Key Applications: Optical communications, LIDAR, THz devices, solar cells

Aluminum Nitride (AlN)

  • • Ultra-wide bandgap (6.2 eV)
  • • Exceptional thermal conductivity (285 W/m·K)
  • • High acoustic velocity for SAW devices
  • • Deep UV LEDs (200-280 nm)
  • • High-temperature electronics
  • • Piezoelectric MEMS devices

Key Applications: UV-C sterilization, RF filters, heat spreaders, sensors

Quantum Dots

  • • Size-tunable emission wavelengths
  • • High quantum yield (>90%)
  • • Narrow emission linewidth
  • • InAs/GaAs for telecom wavelengths
  • • CdSe/ZnS for visible displays
  • • Perovskite QDs for next-gen devices

Key Applications: Quantum computing, single-photon sources, displays, biomarkers
